Interaction Story for Makers

How the browser learned to behave like a living app

Scripting experiments, Ajax buzz, component frameworks, offline-first tooling, and GPU-grade APIs—each milestone asked the same question: “What else can run directly in the browser?” This timeline shares the answers.

Tap a year to open a concise story with context and approachable notes. You will meet the teams who kept stretching what the web platform could do.

Selecting a year opens a dialog in place so you can keep your reading position.

1990s

Scripting and async requests

JavaScript arrived inside the browser, and XMLHttpRequest quietly taught pages to refresh data without a full reload.

2000s

Ajax and JavaScript everywhere

“Ajax” gave asynchronous techniques a name, and Node.js extended JavaScript to the server.

2010s

Components and offline power

React popularized component-based UI, while Service Worker unlocked offline caching and push notifications.

2020s

High-performance web and real-time collaboration

WebAssembly matured, WebGPU exposed the graphics pipeline, and collaboration APIs made the browser feel native.
